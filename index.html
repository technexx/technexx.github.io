About Me:

Hi! I'm a former barista and current Android developer. Back in late 2019, I enrolled in a Udacity coding course on a whim, and within the hour knew I was going to pivot careers.

I used my considerable free time during the pandemic to publish my first app, a text-based game wherein one tries to build the perfect world-destroying creature in their basement laboratory. It was (and is) very silly, but it allowed me to learn a ton of funadmental and advanced Java concepts within the Android framework.

After publishing The Man and the Machine on the Play Store, I wrote and published 4 more apps in 2020, including a pet finder that parsed and returned data on all animals within the Los Angeles County shelter system, and a "pro versus con" weighted list creator. The former taught me about working with and manipulating HTML, and the latter with SQLite and database building.

My most recent project is an integrated timer and calorie tracking app. It allows users to choose from over 350 different activities, using their personal demographics to calculate burned calories based on the activity and its timed duration.

The next step of my journey is to work with a team of developers. I would love to be part of a larger, more integrated project, both for the challenge it provides and the knowledge it imparts.
